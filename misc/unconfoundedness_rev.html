Unconfoundedness and Propensity Scores

Beyond a single RCT

Consider now two RCTs as follows:

Palo Alto non-smoker smoker
treated 152 5
control 2362 122
Geneva non-smoker smoker
treated 581 350
control 2278 1979

To estimate the ATE, we need to estimate treatment effects within each city (i.e. τ̂PA,τ̂GVA\hat{\tau}_{PA}, \hat{\tau}_{GVA}) using the difference-in-means estimator, and then weight the results according to the number of participants in each city

τ̂=26412641+5188τ̂PA+51882641+5188τ̂GVA \hat{\tau}=\frac{2641}{2641+5188}\hat{\tau}_{PA}+\frac{5188}{2641+5188}\hat{\tau}_{GVA}

Palo Alto treatment effect calculation: τ̂PA=5152+5−1222362+122≈−1.7%\hat{\tau}_{PA} = \frac{5}{152 + 5} - \frac{122}{2362 + 122} \approx -1.7\%

Geneva treatment effect calculation: τ̂GVA=350350+581−19792278+1979≈−8.9%\hat{\tau}_{GVA} = \frac{350}{350 + 581} - \frac{1979}{2278 + 1979} \approx -8.9\%

Combined estimate: τ̂=26412641+5188τ̂PA+51882641+5188τ̂GVA≈−6.5%\hat{\tau} = \frac{2641}{2641 + 5188} \hat{\tau}_{PA} + \frac{5188}{2641 + 5188} \hat{\tau}_{GVA} \approx -6.5\%

Stratified estimation

Formalizing this approach, suppose we have covariates in a finite discrete space Xi∈𝒳X_i\in\mathcal{X} and that treatment is random conditionally on XiX_i (i.e. we have an RCT for each group defined by XiX_i).

The assumption of conditional independence (unconfoundedness) is:

{Yi(0),Yi(1)}⟂Di|Xi=x,for all x∈𝒳 \{Y_i(0), Y_i(1)\} \perp D_i | X_i = x, \quad \text{for all } x \in \mathcal{X}

or in more general form: {Yi(0),Yi(1)}⟂Di|Xi\{Y_i(0), Y_i(1)\} \perp D_i | X_i

then the Conditional Average Treatment Effect (CATE) is

τ(x)=𝔼[Yi(1)−Yi(0)|Xi=x] \tau(x) = \mathbb{E}[Y_i(1) - Y_i(0) | X_i = x]

and the stratified estimator is:

τ̂STRAT=∑x∈𝒳nxnτ̂(x) \hat{\tau}_{STRAT} = \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} \frac{n_x}{n} \hat{\tau}(x)

where:

τ̂(x)=1nx,1∑{Xi=x,Di=1}Yi−1nx,0∑{Xi=x,Di=0}Yi=1nx∑{Xi=x}DiYiê(x)−(1−Di)Yi1−ê(x) \begin{align*} \hat{\tau}(x) & =\frac{1}{n_{x,1}}\sum_{\{X_{i}=x,D_{i}=1\}}Y_{i}-\frac{1}{n_{x,0}}\sum_{\{X_{i}=x,D_{i}=0\}}Y_{i}\\ & =\frac{1}{n_{x}}\sum_{\{X_{i}=x\}}\frac{D_{i}Y_{i}}{\hat{e}(x)}-\frac{\left(1-D_{i}\right)Y_{i}}{1-\hat{e}(x)} \end{align*}

Notation:

  • nx=|{i:Xi=x}|n_x = |\{i : X_i = x\}| (number in each strata)
  • nx,d=|{i:Xi=x,Di=d}|n_{x,d} = |\{i : X_i = x, D_i = d\}| (number treated each strata)
  • ê(x)=nx,1nx\hat{e}(x) = \frac{n_{x,1}}{n_x} (estimated probability of treatment)

Theorem 2.1 (Stratified Estimation)

Central limit theorem for stratified estimator: n(τ̂STRAT−τ)⇒N(0,VSTRAT)\sqrt{n}(\hat{\tau}_{STRAT} - \tau) \Rightarrow N(0, V_{STRAT})

Asymptotic variance: VSTRAT=Var[τ(Xi)]+𝔼[σ(1)2(Xi)e(Xi)+σ(0)2(Xi)1−e(Xi)]V_{STRAT} = \text{Var}[\tau(X_i)] + \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{\sigma^2_{(1)}(X_i)}{e(X_i)} + \frac{\sigma^2_{(0)}(X_i)}{1-e(X_i)}\right]

where:

  • e(x)=ℙ[Di=1|Xi=x]e(x) = \mathbb{P}[D_i = 1 | X_i = x] (propensity score)

  • σ(d)2(x)=Var[Yi(d)|Xi=x]\sigma^2_{(d)}(x) = \text{Var}[Y_i^{(d)} | X_i = x]

Decomposition of stratified estimator:

τ̂STRAT=∑x∈𝒳λ(x)τ(x)+∑x∈𝒳(λ̂(x)−λ(x))τ(x)+∑x∈𝒳λ(x)(τ̂(x)−τ(x))+∑x∈𝒳(λ̂(x)−λ(x))(τ̂(x)−τ(x)) \hat{\tau}_{STRAT} = \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} \lambda(x)\tau(x) + \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} (\hat{\lambda}(x) - \lambda(x)) \tau(x) + \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} \lambda(x) (\hat{\tau}(x) - \tau(x)) + \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} (\hat{\lambda}(x) - \lambda(x)) (\hat{\tau}(x) - \tau(x))

where:

  • λ(x)=ℙ[Xi=x]\lambda(x) = \mathbb{P}[X_i = x] (true probability)
  • λ̂(x)=nx/n\hat{\lambda}(x) = n_x/n (empirical probability)

Propensity Score

Definition of propensity score: e(x)=ℙ[Di=1|Xi=x]e(x) = \mathbb{P}[D_i = 1 | X_i = x]

Balancing property: {Yi(0),Yi(1)}⟂Di|e(Xi)\{Y_i(0), Y_i(1)\} \perp D_i | e(X_i)

Proof of balancing property: ℙ[Di=w|{Yi(w)},e(Xi)]=∫𝒳ℙ[Di=w|{Yi(w)},Xi=x]ℙ[Xi=x|{Yi(w)},e(Xi)]dx=∫𝒳ℙ[Di=w|Xi=x]ℙ[Xi=x|{Yi(w)},e(Xi)]dx(unconf.)={e(Xi)if w=11−e(Xi)else\begin{align} \mathbb{P}[D_i = w | \{Y_i(w)\}, e(X_i)] &= \int_{\mathcal{X}} \mathbb{P}[D_i = w | \{Y_i(w)\}, X_i = x] \mathbb{P}[X_i = x | \{Y_i(w)\}, e(X_i)] dx \\ &= \int_{\mathcal{X}} \mathbb{P}[D_i = w | X_i = x] \mathbb{P}[X_i = x | \{Y_i(w)\}, e(X_i)] dx \quad \text{(unconf.)} \\ &= \begin{cases} e(X_i) & \text{if } w = 1 \\ 1 - e(X_i) & \text{else} \end{cases} \end{align}

Propensity Stratification

Sorting step: ê(Xi1)≤ê(Xi2)≤…≤ê(Xin)\hat{e}(X_{i_1}) \leq \hat{e}(X_{i_2}) \leq \ldots \leq \hat{e}(X_{i_n})

Stratum-specific estimator: τ̂j=∑j=⌊(j−1)n/J⌋+1⌊jn/J⌋DiYi∑j=⌊(j−1)n/J⌋+1⌊jn/J⌋Di−∑j=⌊(j−1)n/J⌋+1⌊jn/J⌋(1−Di)Yi∑j=⌊(j−1)n/J⌋+1⌊jn/J⌋(1−Di)\hat{\tau}_j = \frac{\sum_{j=\lfloor(j-1)n/J\rfloor+1}^{\lfloor jn/J\rfloor} D_i Y_i}{\sum_{j=\lfloor(j-1)n/J\rfloor+1}^{\lfloor jn/J\rfloor} D_i} - \frac{\sum_{j=\lfloor(j-1)n/J\rfloor+1}^{\lfloor jn/J\rfloor} (1-D_i) Y_i}{\sum_{j=\lfloor(j-1)n/J\rfloor+1}^{\lfloor jn/J\rfloor} (1-D_i)}

Propensity stratification estimator: τ̂PSTRAT=1J∑j=1Jτ̂j\hat{\tau}_{PSTRAT} = \frac{1}{J} \sum_{j=1}^J \hat{\tau}_j

Inverse-Propensity Weighting (IPW)

IPW estimator with estimated propensity scores: τ̂IPW=1n∑i=1n(DiYiê(Xi)−(1−Di)Yi1−ê(Xi))\hat{\tau}_{IPW} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n \left(\frac{D_i Y_i}{\hat{e}(X_i)} - \frac{(1-D_i) Y_i}{1-\hat{e}(X_i)}\right)

Oracle IPW estimator (true propensity scores): τ̂IPW*=1n∑i=1n(DiYie(Xi)−(1−Di)Yi1−e(Xi))\hat{\tau}^*_{IPW} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n \left(\frac{D_i Y_i}{e(X_i)} - \frac{(1-D_i) Y_i}{1-e(X_i)}\right)

Theorem 2.2 (Oracle IPW)

Central limit theorem for oracle IPW: n(τ̂IPW*−τ)⇒N(0,VIPW*)\sqrt{n}(\hat{\tau}^*_{IPW} - \tau) \Rightarrow N(0, V_{IPW^*})

Asymptotic variance: VIPW*=Var[τ(Xi)]+𝔼[(μ(0)(Xi)+(1−e(Xi))τ(Xi))2e(Xi)(1−e(Xi))]+𝔼[σ(1)2(Xi)e(Xi)+σ(0)2(Xi)1−e(Xi)]V_{IPW^*} = \text{Var}[\tau(X_i)] + \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{(\mu_{(0)}(X_i) + (1-e(X_i))\tau(X_i))^2}{e(X_i)(1-e(X_i))}\right] + \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{\sigma^2_{(1)}(X_i)}{e(X_i)} + \frac{\sigma^2_{(0)}(X_i)}{1-e(X_i)}\right]

Alternative decomposition in proof: DiYie(Xi)−(1−Di)Yi1−e(Xi)=τ(Xi)+(Die(Xi)−1−Di1−e(Xi))c(Xi)+Diεi(1)e(Xi)−(1−Di)εi(0)1−e(Xi)\frac{D_i Y_i}{e(X_i)} - \frac{(1-D_i) Y_i}{1-e(X_i)} = \tau(X_i) + \left(\frac{D_i}{e(X_i)} - \frac{1-D_i}{1-e(X_i)}\right) c(X_i) + \frac{D_i \varepsilon_i^{(1)}}{e(X_i)} - \frac{(1-D_i) \varepsilon_i^{(0)}}{1-e(X_i)}

where: - c(x)=μ(0)(x)+(1−e(x))τ(x)c(x) = \mu_{(0)}(x) + (1-e(x))\tau(x) - εi(w)=Yi(w)−μ(w)(Xi)\varepsilon_i^{(w)} = Y_i^{(w)} - \mu_{(w)}(X_i)

Variance Comparison

Relationship between IPW and stratified variances: VIPW*=VSTRAT+𝔼[(μ(0)(Xi)+(1−e(Xi))τ(Xi))2e(Xi)(1−e(Xi))]V_{IPW^*} = V_{STRAT} + \mathbb{E}\left[\frac{(\mu_{(0)}(X_i) + (1-e(X_i))\tau(X_i))^2}{e(X_i)(1-e(X_i))}\right]

Therefore: VIPW*≥VSTRATV_{IPW^*} \geq V_{STRAT} (IPW is generally less efficient than stratification)

Overlap Assumptions

Weak overlap: 𝔼[1/(e(Xi)(1−e(Xi)))]<∞\mathbb{E}[1/(e(X_i)(1-e(X_i)))] < \infty

Strong overlap: η≤e(x)≤1−ηfor all x∈𝒳\eta \leq e(x) \leq 1-\eta \quad \text{for all } x \in \mathcal{X}

for some η>0\eta > 0.

Connection Between Stratified and IPW

When 𝒳\mathcal{X} is discrete, the stratified estimator can be written as IPW: τ̂STRAT=1n∑i=1n(DiYiê(Xi)−(1−Di)Yi1−ê(Xi))\hat{\tau}_{STRAT} = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n \left(\frac{D_i Y_i}{\hat{e}(X_i)} - \frac{(1-D_i) Y_i}{1-\hat{e}(X_i)}\right)

where: ê(x)=nx1nx\hat{e}(x) = \frac{n_{x1}}{n_x}

Auxiliary Functions and Notation

Conditional mean functions: μ(w)(x)=𝔼[Yi(w)|Xi=x]\mu^{(w)}(x) = \mathbb{E}[Y_i^{(w)} | X_i = x]

Conditional variance functions: σ(w)2(x)=Var[Yi(w)|Xi=x]\sigma^{(w)2}(x) = \text{Var}[Y_i^{(w)} | X_i = x]

Sample sizes by stratum and treatment: - nx=|{i:Xi=x}|n_x = |\{i : X_i = x\}| (total in stratum xx) - nx1=|{i:Xi=x,Di=1}|n_{x1} = |\{i : X_i = x, D_i = 1\}| (treated in stratum xx)
- nx0=|{i:Xi=x,Di=0}|n_{x0} = |\{i : X_i = x, D_i = 0\}| (control in stratum xx)

Estimation Error Terms

Decomposition of stratified estimator: τ̂STRAT=∑x∈𝒳λ(x)τ(x)+∑x∈𝒳(λ̂(x)−λ(x))τ(x)+∑x∈𝒳λ(x)(τ̂(x)−τ(x))+∑x∈𝒳(λ̂(x)−λ(x))(τ̂(x)−τ(x))\hat{\tau}_{STRAT} = \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} \lambda(x)\tau(x) + \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} (\hat{\lambda}(x) - \lambda(x)) \tau(x) + \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} \lambda(x) (\hat{\tau}(x) - \tau(x)) + \sum_{x \in \mathcal{X}} (\hat{\lambda}(x) - \lambda(x)) (\hat{\tau}(x) - \tau(x))

where: - λ(x)=ℙ[Xi=x]\lambda(x) = \mathbb{P}[X_i = x] (true probability) - λ̂(x)=nx/n\hat{\lambda}(x) = n_x/n (empirical probability)
